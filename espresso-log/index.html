☕ Aloha, I Need Espresso

A daily dispatch from Hawaii, where everything is perfect except the coffee situation.

Day 1

No caffeine. None. Zero. The day happened anyway, which I found presumptuous.

At home there is a machine that knows exactly what I need. It does not ask questions. It does not offer alternatives. It produces 25ml of something that makes the morning make sense, and it does this without being asked twice.

That machine is not here.

I am.

We'll see how this goes.

Day 2

My body sent a strongly worded memo today in the form of a headache. The subject line was "Where Is It." I have no answer for it yet.

Groceries were purchased. Very on-brand vacation activity.

The real headline: a spicy ahi poke bowl, eaten on the beach, surrounded by my wife and kids. Genuinely great. My brain still found a way to quietly file a complaint about the lack of espresso, even mid-bite. Impressive dedication, honestly.

Everyone else is having a wonderful time. I am too. I'd just also like a shot of espresso, is all.

Day 3

I caved. Not to espresso — to a Red Bull. The headache won. It wasn't pride, it was survival, and I'd like that on the record.

It worked, in the sense that I remained conscious. It is not espresso. We both know this. Moving on.

Another poke bowl happened, because apparently this is just what I eat now and I have no complaints.

Then the real event: an entire day at the beach. Huge waves, beautiful day, and as an island boy I was fully in my element out there. The kids had a tougher time keeping up in the surf but made up for it building sandcastles and watching some new family members go fish, which apparently was its own kind of entertainment. My wife took the wiser path — sun, drink, no waves. I rode as many as I could.

Capped the day off riding back in the bed of a pickup truck, which is somehow always better than it has any right to be.

Still no espresso. Genuinely didn't think about it once until just now, writing this. Progress, maybe.

Day 4

Red Bull, again. We're two for two now. The espresso machine at home remains blissfully unaware it's being replaced by a beverage primarily associated with extreme sports and bad decisions.

Ordinary day on paper, great day in practice. Kailua's North Beach delivered — gorgeous beachside park, coral findings, and mountain views that made me stop and take more scenic photos than any one phone should hold. The mountains here just generally show off, and I respect it.

Capped things off at an ice cream shop called "Please Come Again," which is either a great name or a mild threat, depending on how the visit goes. Mine went great: matcha coconut. Daughter went with rose lychee and loved every bite. Wife found a local chocolate flavor worth bragging about. One son ordered shiso mint chocolate chip and may have quietly won the day. Other son: more chocolate, no notes, no regrets.

Drove back through Kailua. Good day, no asterisks needed.

Day 5

Father's Day. In case the title of this log didn't make it obvious enough — yes, I am, in fact, a father. Several of them depend on me. It's going fine.

No espresso update, because today had bigger things going on. We drove out to Kahuku, and the views on the way were the kind that make you stop narrating your own vacation and just look out the window for a while.

Landed at Mālaekahana State Recreation Area, a different beach entirely — much livelier underwater. Snorkeled around without an actual snorkel, just the mask, which I can now confirm is significantly harder than advertised when you have to surface for air every ten seconds. Saw a turtle, plenty of fish, a sea urchin, and some spearfishing happening nearby. Also had what might be the best poke bowl of the trip so far, which is a bold claim given the competition.

Kids gave the wavy water another go and struggled valiantly, as expected, in the most wholesome way possible.

Finished at Kahuku's famous shrimp spot, which, respectfully, undersold itself — the shrimp wasn't quite my thing, but the steak was genuinely excellent and deserves its own reputation. Drove back, relaxed for the rest of the evening, details fuzzy. Good kind of fuzzy.

Happy Father's Day to me, I guess.

Day 6

Monday. Chill day, allegedly. I'm a researcher, which means summer students exist, which means vacation comes with a small asterisk most years. Not this year, though — I checked in, made sure nothing was on fire, science continued without me having to actually be there. Remote supervision from a beach state. Living the dream, slightly.

Red Bull, again, standing in for the role espresso used to play. It's not winning any awards but it's reliable, which is more than I can say for my relationship with it.

Real highlight: barbecuing — specifically Korean BBQ ribs, which were a genuinely nice change of pace from beach days and poke bowls.

Still no espresso. The streak continues.
